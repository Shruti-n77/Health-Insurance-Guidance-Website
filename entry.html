    Health Maintenance Organization (HMO) Plan: An HMO plan typically requires you to choose a primary care physician (PCP) who will act as your first point of contact for all your health needs. You'll need a referral from your PCP to see specialists or other healthcare providers.
  1. Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) Plan: With a PPO plan, you have more flexibility to choose your healthcare providers. You can see any provider within the PPO network, but you'll pay less if you stay within the network.
  2. Point of Service (POS) Plan: A POS plan is similar to an HMO plan in that you choose a PCP who coordinates your care. However, you may have the option to see out-of-network providers, but you'll likely pay more for those services.
  3. Exclusive Provider Organization (EPO) Plan: With an EPO plan, you must use providers within the network to receive coverage. However, you typically don't need a referral to see a specialist.
  4. High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP): A HDHP plan usually has lower monthly premiums but higher deductibles. You'll need to pay a certain amount out-of-pocket before your insurance kicks in.
  5. Catastrophic Health Plan: A catastrophic health plan is designed to cover major medical expenses, such as hospitalization or surgery. These plans typically have low monthly premiums but high deductibles.
